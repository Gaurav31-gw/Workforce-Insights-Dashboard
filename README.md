# 📊 Workforce Insights Dashboard (Excel)

## 🧾 Project Overview

The **Workforce Insights Dashboard** is an interactive HR analytics project developed in **Microsoft Excel** to analyze employee data and uncover key patterns related to workforce distribution and attrition.

This dashboard transforms raw HR data into **actionable insights**, enabling better decision-making for employee retention and organizational planning.

---

## 🎯 Business Objective

The goal of this project is to:

* Identify **key drivers of employee attrition**
* Analyze workforce demographics and distribution
* Provide insights to improve **employee retention strategies**
* Build a **dynamic and interactive dashboard** using Excel

## 📈 Dashboard Features

### 👥 Workforce Distribution

* Gender-based employee segmentation using donut charts
* Helps understand diversity in the organization

### 🎓 Education-wise Attrition

* Attrition comparison across education levels
* Highlights which qualification groups are more likely to leave

### 🏢 Department-wise Attrition

* Distribution across HR, R&D, and Sales
* Identifies departments with higher attrition concentration

### 👔 Role-based Attrition

* Treemap visualization of attrition across job roles
* Useful for identifying role-specific retention issues

### 📊 Age Group Analysis

* Attrition trends segmented by age groups
* Helps understand which age bracket is most affected

### 💍 Marital Status Insights

* Attrition comparison across marital categories
* Provides behavioral patterns linked to personal demographics

### 🎛️ Interactive Filters

* Slicers for:

  * Education Field
  * Department
* Enables dynamic and user-driven analysis

## 🔍 Key Insights & Findings

### 1️⃣ Workforce Composition

* The organization has a **male-dominated workforce (~60%)**
* Female representation is comparatively lower, indicating a potential area for diversity improvement

### 2️⃣ Attrition Overview

* The **attrition rate of 16.12%** is moderately high
* Indicates a need for **retention strategies and employee engagement initiatives**

### 3️⃣ Education Impact

* Employees with a **Bachelor’s degree show the highest attrition (99 cases)**
* Suggests early-career professionals are more likely to switch jobs

### 4️⃣ Department Analysis

* **R&D department contributes the highest share (~56%) of attrition**
* Sales follows, while HR shows minimal attrition
* Indicates possible workload, growth, or management issues in R&D

### 5️⃣ Age Group Trends

* Highest attrition observed in **25–34 age group (112 employees)**
* This aligns with career-switch phase → employees actively explore opportunities

### 6️⃣ Role-based Risk

* Roles like **Laboratory Technician and Sales Executive** show higher attrition
* These roles may require:

  * Better compensation
  * Career progression paths
  * Work-life balance improvements

### 7️⃣ Marital Status Pattern

* **Married employees show the highest attrition (673)**
* Followed by Single employees
* Indicates external responsibilities or job stability expectations might influence decisions
